Home Economics

Home economics, often known as domestic science or family and consumer sciences, is a branch of study that focuses on consumer issues, housing and interior design, food preparation and nutrition, textiles and apparel, and human development. Home economics teaches students how to cook properly while also instilling responsibility. You will learn how to assess information using facts, surveys, and statistics so that you can build cooking skills and make informed food decisions based on nutrition, economics, culture, ethics, and sustainability.
